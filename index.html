Yes — that's the entire point. After an engineer does the one-time setup (~30 minutes), PMs and marketers log into a plain dashboard where they can read, edit, and test AI instructions in plain language. No code, no terminal required. Engineers approve before anything goes live, so there's no risk of accidental breakage.

An AI prompt is the text that tells an AI model how to behave. For example: "You are a helpful customer support agent. Be warm and professional." That text determines everything about how your AI responds. Right now, most teams have this buried in their code — which means only engineers can change it. PromptOps moves it to a shared dashboard.

A PM or marketer edits the AI instructions, runs a quality test (score 0–10), and clicks "Request Approval." The engineer gets a notification, reviews what changed and the score, and either approves or leaves feedback. If approved, the change goes live in ~10 seconds — no deployment needed.

All of them. PromptOps works with GPT-4o, Claude, Gemini, Mistral, Llama, and more. It also works with LangChain, LlamaIndex, and raw API calls. If your team is using any AI model, PromptOps can manage its instructions.

PromptOps is operational infrastructure. Think of it like the difference between a Google Doc and a proper deployment pipeline.
